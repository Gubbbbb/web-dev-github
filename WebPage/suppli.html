Supplies Needed for Taxidermy

Mounts

In order to understand what tools you need and why, you first need to split the tools into three categories. Cutting, preserving, and styling. Most tools will be in the cutting and styling categories.

Cutting

  • Scalpel (and replacement blades)
  • X-acto Knife (explanable for itself)
  • Cutting Pliers (for joints and bones)
  • Round Pliers (for gripping and wire bending)
  • Disposable Gloves (a must have, make sure to get the right size)

Perserving

  • Borax (sodium borate)

Styling

  • Modeling Clay
  • Carving Tools
  • Plaster of Paris
  • Foam Form (or craft foam + rasp for shaping)
  • Wire (size varies)
  • Tweezers
  • Wire Cutters
  • Thread
  • Needle
  • Artifical Eyes (size, color, etc. based on animal)
  • Mounting Board

Most of these supplies are easily available in your local hardware and craft store besides the artificial eyes and foam forms. Taxidermy itself require a fairly steady hand, and starting off you should be doing smaller mammals, then slowly making your way up as you get more comfortable with the tools you're using. Videos on skinning and mounting a rat can help guide you through this proccess. Fleshing larger animals though will require a Fleshing Beam and a Pallet Knife. Or, if you perfer not to do it manual forever, you can use a Fleshing Machine.

Tanning Your Hide for a Mount

Though tanning requires similar materials, there is a few additional materials needed for this process.

Tanning Hide/Fur

  • Disinfectant (i.e Lysol)
  • Denatured Alcohol
  • Heavy Rubber Gloves
  • Basil or Pail
  • Formic or Acetic Acid
  • Soda Ash or Baking Soda (for basification)
  • PH Testing Strips
  • Aqueous Degreaser (only really needed for greasy animals)
  • Tanning Oil
  • Non-iodized salt (NOT rock salt NO rock salt)

What About Fish Mounts?

Fish mounts themselves are a delicate challenge. With fish you have to preserve the head and gills, where as with regular mounts most of the time you can discard everything besides the hide. There are videos online showing how fish are fleshed and skinned for mounting.

Preparing Fish Skin

  • Tweezers(get the eyes out)
  • Fish Cheek Scraper
  • Fish Skinning Knife
  • Fish Skinning Shears
  • Fish skin Scraper

For preserving fish skin all you need is a mixture of half borax and half water and to soak it overnight.

Mounting

  • Mache
  • Foam for Carving
  • Hide Adhesive/Paste
  • Staple Gun
  • Cardboard
  • Mesh
  • Large Paper Clips

Bird Mounts

Even if bird mounts need a bit more care afterwards, all tools for mounting them are similar to the ones used on a small mammal. Though, there are a few more things needed for preventing wing shrinkage and posing the wings themselves.

Preventing Shrinkage

  • Injecting fluid
  • Hypodermic needle

Posing

  • Drill
  • Pipe Cleaner
  • Heavy Gauge Wire
  • Electrical Tape
  • Caulking
  • Bird Tweezers

Wet Specimens

Please note that I do not include the steps on this page of actually making wet specimens. All I will be putting here is the tools needed for making the specimen. I recommend finding a guide in order to learn further before pursuing creating a wet specimen.

First Stage

  • Respirator(ALWAYS wear one)
  • Powder-free Nitrile Gloves
  • Puppy Pee Pad (with plastic backing)
  • Hypodermic Needle
  • Syringe
  • Fixative(typically formalin)
  • Glass Jar(HAS to be glass and properly sealed)

After you have all steps required done for the first stage, and have waited the allotted time, move onto stage two. Make sure to repeat stage two twice.

Second Stage

  • Isopropyl 70% (or ethanol)
